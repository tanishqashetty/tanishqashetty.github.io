What the research revealed
  • Every creator I interviewed had access to retention data. None had a workflow to turn it into a creative decision
  • The gap isn't laziness — it's translation. The data lives as a graph; what's needed is a brief
  • Three creators said they close the analytics tab "feeling vague guilt" — they know something is wrong but can't act on it
  • Four creators said multiple AI suggestions felt paralyzing — one specific brief per video was the right constraint
How the agent works
  • 48 hours after posting, the agent reads the retention curve — automatic, no creator action required
  • Creator inputs three things: their niche, video concept, and retention curve in plain language — "strong open, 40% drop at 0:35, spike at 1:10, steady decline after"
  • Claude diagnoses the structural issue, pattern-matches against what works in that niche, and outputs a targeted brief: hook format, optimal reveal timing, where the re-engagement moment should land
  • The brief surfaces inside the Inspiration tab — the Inspiration tab is about new ideas, Creative Replay makes the next idea better-informed by the last one
Why it's named Creative Replay
  • You review the tape. You figure out what to do differently. You go again
  • It's the post-game analysis that the Inspiration tab doesn't do — that tab is about discovering new ideas, this is about getting better at executing the idea you already had
  • Creator language, not product language. Every creator understands reviewing a replay
Key product decisions
  • Agent, not a feature — it triggers automatically; no creator should have to remember to open analytics 48 hours after posting
  • Retention curve as plain language input — the prototype doesn't need real API data; a creator describing their curve in one sentence is enough signal for diagnosis
  • Output is a brief, not a report — hook format, reveal timing, re-engagement placement. Specific, not general
  • One brief per video, one metric to track — the constraint forces prioritization and makes the learning loop testable across videos
  • Guardrail: creative confidence score — the brief must feel like theirs, not the tool's. If creators feel prescribed to, it fails regardless of retention outcomes
